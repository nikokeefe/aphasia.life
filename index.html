Aphasia

a.pha.sia (ay-faze-yuh) n. Partial or total loss of the ability to articulate ideas or comprehend spoken or written language, resulting from damage to the brain caused by injury or disease (Aphasia New Zealand Charitable trust, 2010).

Aphasia.life

We created this website for people with aphasia, their families and friends and community, to improve the communication environment of people with aphasia. We aim to provide you with easily accessible information and any further resources you may need to help you on your communication journey.

Improving your communication enviroment

  • For people with Aphasia talking with others can be difficult.

  • • People may talk too fast.

     

  • • People may talk too loud.

  • • It can be hard to find words.

  • • It can be hard to read words and numbers

  • • It can be hard to understand someone talking

Helping you to talk with other people

  • Talking to people with Aphasia can be difficult.

  • • Don't talk too fast.

     

  • • Give them time to answer.

  • • Don't act like they are dumb.

  • • Don't be afraid to try.

  • • Smile!

Support for your communication

  • There are lots of ways to help you to Talk.

  • • From a pen and paper.

     

  • • To picture boards.

  • • Apps for your phone.

  • • I-Pads and talking devices (AAC).

  • • Or use Photos and pictures.
